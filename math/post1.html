What is the Riemann Zeta Function?

An introduction to one of the most mysterious functions in mathematics

Definition

The Riemann zeta function is defined as:

\[ \zeta(s) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^s} \]

for complex numbers \(s\) with real part greater than 1.

Importance

This function encodes deep information about the distribution of prime numbers through its connection with the Euler product:

\[ \zeta(s) = \prod_{p\ \text{prime}} \left(1 - p^{-s}\right)^{-1} \]

Riemann Hypothesis

One of the most famous unsolved problems in mathematics concerns the zeros of \(\zeta(s)\). The Riemann Hypothesis asserts that all nontrivial zeros lie on the critical line \(\text{Re}(s) = 1/2\).
